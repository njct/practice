Diabetes

Make sure to speak with your physician today for any concerns you may have with managing your disease and mediciations.


Type 2 diabetes is a long-term disease where your body does not make enough of a hormone called insulin, your body does not respond well to the insulin that it does make, or a combination of there things. Insulin is needed to move the sugar (glucose) from your blood into your body's cells where it can be used as a source of energy.


When you have type 2 diabetes, keeping you blood sugar in a health range is important. You can help do this with:


  • Nutrition
  • Exericse
  • Lifestyle changes
  • Medicines and/or insulin, if needed
  • Support from your doctors and others

How to stay aware of blood sugar

  • Check your blood sugar as often as your doctor recommends
  • Have your A1c(hemoglobin A1c) level checked two or more times a year as directed by your doctor. A1c measures your average blood sugar level over the past 3 months. Your doctor will help set personal treatment goals for you.

How to manage high and low blood sugar

Low blood sugar is called hypoglycemia. This is when blood sugar is at or below 70 mg/dl


Signs may include: feeling hungry, worried or nervous, sweaty and clammy, confused, dizzy, sleepy, sick to your stomach. Having a fast heartbeat, a headeche, change in vision, tingling or no feeling around your mouth, lips, or tounge.

Treating low blood sugar

To treat low blood sugar, cossider following the 15:15 rule:

  • Take 15 grams of a fast-acting card (carbohydrate). Talk with your doctor about how much you should take.
  • Some fast acting cards are:
    • Sugar tablets (gloucose pills). Take 304 pills
    • 6-8 oz (120-150 mL) of fruit juice or regular (not diet) soda
    • 1 Tbsp (15 mL) honey or sugar
  • Recheck blood sugar 15 minutes after taking 15 grams of a fast-acting carb ( carbohydrate ). If blood sugar is not >70 mg/dL, repeat the above steps.

Treating very low blood sugar

If your blood sugar is at or below 54mg/dL(3mmol/L), you have very low blood sugar(severe hypoglycemia).This is an emergency.Get medical help right away. Call your local emergency services(911).
